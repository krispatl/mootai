The problem

Traditional moot court is limited by time, room availability, and uneven feedback. Remote tools weren’t built for courtroom procedure, so teams improvise with generic video apps.

  • Scheduling and space constraints limit practice frequency.
  • No shared simulation state—participants get out of sync.
  • Feedback is subjective, delayed, or lost.

The solution

Moot AI brings a procedural, role-aware courtroom to the web with real-time voice, avatar presence, synchronized evidence, and AI assistance tuned for pedagogy.

  • Role assignment & turn-taking baked into the session.
  • Browser-native voice and presence—no installs, just a link.
  • AI coaching and transcripts aligned to rubrics.

Design notes

  • Authoritative state: Colyseus keeps roles, timers, objections, and exhibit pointers canonical.
  • Voice path: Peer connections over WebRTC; TURN for tough networks.
  • Coach: NLP service consumes live transcript and emits rubric-aligned hints.
  • Data: Exhibits store in object storage; session logs export as PDF/CSV.

FAQ

Answers to common questions
Do participants need to install anything?

No. Moot AI is browser-native. Share a link, choose a role, and you’re in.

How does the AI coach work?

The system analyzes live transcripts for structure, clarity, and references, then surfaces rubric-aligned hints after or during sessions (configurable).

Is this suitable for graded assessments?

Yes—export session logs, timestamps, and scores. Rubrics are configurable per course.

What about privacy and compliance?

Rooms can be invite-only; transcripts and recordings are optional. We support GDPR-friendly data retention controls.
